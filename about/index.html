About

Melissa Riedel, born on the 19th of December 1994 in Berlin, is a Pianist and Composer.  When she was 4 years old she began to play violin. In the age of 5 she had her first concerts. She immediately fell in love with music. Music is her great passion and she just knew that music will be her future.  In the age of 9 she started to play in the orchestra and one year later she fell in love with the piano. She couldn’t stop to play anymore so big was her love. In that time she improvised a lot and composed her first music.When she was 11 years old she got her first piano lesson. Willing to learn everything she was immersed in the sound world of her piano.   Her piano teacher gave her intensiv lessons and inspired her to become a pianist.  When she was 12 years old she gave her first piano concert and in the age of 14 she participated the first time in Germany`s biggest competition for young musicians ’’Jugend musiziert’’.  Since then she won several first prizes.  When she was 18 years old she won the highest prize for the best interpretation of Beethoven's Sonata for Piano and Violincello op.5 no. 2 in g-minor. As a composer she writes music for orchestra, piano and violin.
